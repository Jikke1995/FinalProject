Plastic pollution of the oceans

Even though worldwide plastic pollution is a well-known problem, every year more than 8 million tons of plastic is being dumped in the ocean. The production of plastic has risen for more than 300 million tonnes since 1970. Future perspectives show that this will only become more and more. More plastic generation will automatically lead to more plastic waste, but that doesn't have to mean that this waste will end in our oceans. There are several ways (recycling, combusting with Energy Recovery) to manage the usage and re-usage of plastic. But for now the rates of these ways of managing plastic waste are still to low. One of the reasons is that big companies (responsible for a lot of plastic waste) have high power and for them it can be expensive to recycle their plastic waste instead of dumping it. There are only a few small (in comparison) organisations that take action. Most important, there's a lack of awareness in general. People need to become more aware of the size of the problem of plastic pollution and what might happen if nothing is going to change.


About this project

The datavisualisations on this website will inform you about the numbers of plastic waste all around the world. First it displays the plastig generation over the last 60 years, to see how much that has grown. Next, it gives you insights in what the share of each country is in the global plastic waste production, and how much of their own plastic waste they manage/mismanage. Lastly, it shows the amount of plastic particles floating in the global oceans. The aim of these visualisations is to help you realise the magnitude of the problem of plastic genetartion, waste and pollution and it will hopefully inspire you to do something about is yourself.
