Worldwide plastics production

First of all, to understand the size of the problem of plastic pollution, it's necessary to realise how rapid the growth of global plastic production has been over the last 65 years. When the first completely synthetic plastic was produced in 1907 by Leo Baekland, it took until about 1950 before the worldwide plastics production increased explosively. About that time the world produced 2 million tonnes of plastic per year, but it increased nearly 200-fold to 381 million tonnes in 2015.

Worldwide plastic waste generation

Prior to 1980 the percentage of plastic that was recycled was negligible, almost 100% was discarded. Since 1970 rates increased on average by about 0.7% per year. That means that in 2015 more or less 20% of the plastic waste was recycled. Besides the 25% that was incinerated, 55% of the global plastic waste was discarded. Plastic pollution of the oceans

Plastic waste that is mismanaged means that it is either littered or inadequately disposed. It is at high risk of entering the ocean via rivers where it has been dumped, via wind when it has been dumped somewhere without watching, or tidal transport. But when it enters the oceans, where does it go? Big soups of plastic arise due to oceanic surface currents and wind patterns, because of the buoyancy of most of the plastic particles. These are called gyres. In the figure below you can see the amount of surface plastic particles of each ocean all over the world.

Surface plastic particles by ocean basins (2013)

Quantity of plastic waste particles floating at the ocean surface, counted independent of size, in billions.

This visualisation doesn't even show the plastic waste in oceans more deeply. As you can see by these big amounts of plastic particles floating around, the plastic pollution isn't a problem you can easily resolve. And it isn't about cleaning the oceans, it's about dealing with the cause of the pollution. It's about awareness of the generation, usage and wasting of plastics.
